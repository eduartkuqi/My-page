Nelson Mandela

..a dreamer who never gives up

Early Life

  • 1918 - Born on in the village of Mvezo in Umtata
  • 1933 - Mandela began his secondary education at Clarkebury Methodist High School in Engcobo
  • 1939 - Mandela began work on a BA degree at the University of Fort Hare

Revolutionary Life

  • 1943–1949 - Mandela began studying law at the University of the Witwatersrand, where he was the only black African student and faced racism
  • 1950 - He was elected national president of the ANCYL and started protest against apartheid and white minority rule.
  • 1962-1964 - Mandela was captured by police along with fellow activist Cecil Williams near Howick. Mandela and his co-accused were transferred from Pretoria to the prison on Robben Island, remaining there for the next 18 years.

Political Life

  • 1988 Mandela's 70th birthday attracted international attention, including a tribute concert at London's Wembley Stadium that was televised and watched by an estimated 200 million viewers
  • 1989 - Botha after a meeting with Mandela, was replaced as state president by de Klerk six weeks later; the new president believed that apartheid was unsustainable and released a number of ANC prisoners.
  • 1990–91 - Mandela proceeded on an African tour, meeting supporters and politicians in Zambia, Zimbabwe, Namibia, Libya and Algeria, and continuing to Europe. Encouraging foreign countries to support sanctions against the apartheid government
  • 1994 - ANC won a sweeping victory, taking 63% of the vote and though the ANC's victory assured Mandela election as president.
  • 1994-1999 - Presiding over the transition from apartheid minority rule to a multicultural democracy, Mandela saw national reconciliation as the primary task of his presidency. Under Mandela's presidency, welfare spending increased by 13% in 1996/97, 13% in 1997/98, and 7% in 1998/99.
